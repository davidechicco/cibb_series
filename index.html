CIBB Conferences on Computational Intelligence Methods for Bioinformatics and Biostatistics

CIBB is an international annual scientific conference on computational intelligence and computational statistics applied to bioinformatics, biostatistics, medical informatics, and cheminformatics.
           

The main goal of the CIBB international conferences is to provide a multidisciplinary forum for researchers interested in applying computational intelligence, in a broad sense, to open problems in bioinformatics, biostatistics, systems and synthetic biology, medical informatics, and cheminformatics. Cutting-edge methodologies capable of accelerating discoveries in the life sciences, as well as the results of studies in these areas, are discussed by participants each year during the conference. Following its tradition and roots, this scientific meeting brings together researchers from the international community who are interested in advancements and future perspectives in the applications of computer science, machine learning, and computational statistics to biology, medicine, healthcare, and chemistry. Moreover, given current trends and future opportunities at the interface between computing and the life sciences, the application of computational intelligence to systems and synthetic biology—and its resulting impact on innovative medicine—are of particular interest to the conference. Theoretical and experimental biologists are also invited to participate in order to present novel challenges and foster multidisciplinary collaboration, including new directions related to theoretical computer science.
Participants of the confence usually include university professors, researchers, students, research centers scientists, and professionals from industries and biomedical companies. After each year's conference, its peer-reviewed articles are published in the proceedings volumes of Springer Lecture Notes in Computer Science (LNCS) and selected extended peer-reviewed articles are published in supplements or special issues of journals such as BMC Bioinformatics, BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making, Soft Computing, Artificial Intelligence in Medicine, or International Journal of Approximate Reasoning.

     
The CIBB conference guarantees the high quality of the scientific papers presented through a rigorous double peer review process. First, short papers are peer-reviewed by anonymous experts which decide if they are accepted for the conference or rejected. And after the conference, the extended articles based on the short papers undergo a second peer review process which decide if they can be published in the post-proceedings books, in the supplement journals, or rejected. Therefore, if an article is ultimately published in a CIBB journal supplement or in a CIBB proceedings volume, it means it has survived two processes of anonymous peer review. The CIBB conference proceedings have been published by Springer within the Springer Lecture Notes in Computer Science (LNCS) book series, and are currently indexed in Scopus, Web of Science Conference Proceedings Citation Index, Semantic Scholar, Google Scholar, and other bibliography article databases.
     
The CIBB conferences support the principles of open science by encouraging article athors to use open source programming languages (such as R or Python), to openly publish their software code and their data, and to publish articles in open access journals. The CIBB scientific meetings have always attracted a high number of young participants and women: the CIBB 2025 conference in Milan was attended by 260 participants, with 42% being women.

Conference history

The International Meeting on Computational Intelligence Methods for Bioinformatics and Biostatistics (CIBB) conference series was originally founded by Roberto Tagliaferri (Università di Salerno, Italy, EU), Francesco Masulli (Università di Genova, Italy, EU), and Antonina Starita (Università di Pisa, Italy, EU), who formed its initial steering committee in 2004. The conference began as a special session of the 14th Italian Workshop on Neural Networks (WIRN 2004) in Perugia (Italy, EU), and was a special session of international conferences organized in Italy for its first four editions. CIBB became an independent conference in October 2008 with its first autonomous edition held in Vietri sul Mare (Italy, EU), just a few months after sad passing of the CIBB founder Antonina Starita (1939-2008).

Since 2004, the CIBB conference series has generated 15 proceedings books, 4 sections of proceedings books, and 10 journal supplements, which together contain approximately 450 peer-reviewed original scientific publications. To date, approximately 1,600 participants have attended the CIBB conferences.
The CIBB 2026 upcoming international meeting, scheduled to be held in Rome (Italy, EU) in September 2026, will mark the 21st edition of the CIBB conference.
